Introduction

My names Ruby Quigley. My projects about the "STARS" site. During my week one analysis and creation of flows as part of project work, I had to only create one flow diagram. I created one where it details the process of . I created a flow detailing what caused the user to trigger this and what it looks like at the end. I included what action is using within the step of the flow and the systems response to the action. The users goal is explained with the pain point at said step being inlcluded to help with the research. My flow is important to the user as it logs what the user is doing, why and how is it going wrong for the user. This is important as these all influence the experience the user has when interacting with the "STARS" site to upload.

4 View assignment A check list of the assignment aspects and what’s needed in the submission. Check the checklist in relation to the work they’ve done to proceed with submission process The pain point here is when you've completed the checklist there's no clear progression button, there's no clear indication that you must click the next step section of the progression bar to be taken to the next section of the submission process. 5 Create/edit my work Here the cover letter option will be selected and a selection of what to upload and its title will now appear to be edited by the user. To include files and details for their submission to progress The pain point here being the naming of cover letter as leaving a note. There is a second pain point here, when adding screenshots to the submission, the page begins to looked cramped and irritating. The title selection is also confusing. A prompt of instructions would make it easier for the user. E.g. Tile : - Add a title to header your work.
6 Select Goals for Review A check list is loaded to select what goals to review To load the checklist for the user to select what will be reviewed The pain point here is the ability to write the cover letter in this section to skip when done, this can cause a first time user to be confused and trying to go back through sections of the website leaving them frustrated
7 Self Review A compilation of the editing and what your uploading is shown, allowing the user to self-review and edit in this section. To load the users, work and progress, allowing them to review and edit. Letting them complete a checklist to proceed Depending on what the user has edited and uploaded this section can look squashed and formatted in a non-appeasing way to a user. The self-review checklist down at the bottom can be deceiving as its not clearly indicated that the user must check the boxes and make comments to pass to the next stage.
8 Request AI Feedback Keeps the same page as the self-review but instead of self-review complete t now shows ai feedback request as a selection. Navigate the reviews in order to get AI feedback on what they submitted. The AI isn’t entirely accurate. The ability to see the AI’s review is difficult to find as it isn’t indicated that you need to press get ai review, then when it’s got it displays review received. Its not indicated that you need to click Review received by AI reviewer in order to see the review.
9 Submit Final Keeps the same page as ai feedback but instead with a small section dedicated to submit final artefact. To do their final progression and submit their final artefact and all its attachments Its difficult to see where you must submit the final artefact as a small section to do so is loaded underneath all the work the user has done in the same page style as self-review and ai feedback. The submission is only a small section with a button on the other side of the screen, making it harder to notice and see.
